Help to Buy ISA Closed to new accounts

The Help to Buy ISA scheme closed to new applicants on 30 November 2019. If you already have one, you can typically continue paying in until 30 November 2029 and use the government bonus when buying your first home (bonus claim deadlines apply). Information only — not financial advice.

How a Help to Buy ISA Works (for existing holders)

  • You save cash and earn interest tax-free within the ISA wrapper.
  • When you buy your first qualifying home with a mortgage, a 25% government bonus can be added to your savings (up to scheme limits).
  • There are monthly subscription limits and rules on how/when the bonus is claimed via your conveyancer.

Exact limits, deadlines and provider rules apply. If unsure, check your provider’s documentation and official guidance.

Help to Buy ISA vs Lifetime ISA vs Cash ISA

Feature Help to Buy ISA Lifetime ISA (LISA) Cash ISA
Status Closed to new accounts; existing holders can keep using per scheme rules. Open to eligible savers (18–39 to open; contribute until 50). Open to eligible savers (16+ for Cash ISA).
Main Purpose First-home purchase (with a bonus). First-home purchase or retirement (with a bonus). Tax-free savings (no bonus).
Government Bonus 25% bonus on eligible savings for a qualifying first-home purchase (caps/limits apply). 25% bonus on contributions (up to £1,000 per year on £4,000 contributions). None.
Property Value Limit Limit applies under scheme rules for eligible first homes. Up to £450,000 for first-home purchases. Not applicable.
Withdrawals Normal ISA rules for withdrawals; bonus paid via conveyancer at purchase. Tax-free at 60 or for first home (after 12 months). Other withdrawals face a 25% charge. Depends on account type (easy access vs fixed-rate).
Allowance Monthly and overall limits per scheme (for existing holders). £4,000/year (counts towards £20,000 total ISA allowance). Counts toward total ISA allowance (£20,000/year across all ISAs).
Risk Cash savings (no market risk; rates vary). Cash or Stocks & Shares LISA (cash has no market risk; investments can fall as well as rise). Cash savings (no market risk; rates vary).

This table is a simplified comparison for information only. Product rules and eligibility apply.

Key Considerations (Information Only)

  • Help to Buy ISA accounts can’t be newly opened, but existing holders may continue under scheme rules and timelines.
  • Lifetime ISAs carry a withdrawal charge if used for reasons other than a first home (after 12 months) or from age 60.
  • Cash ISAs don’t offer a bonus but keep interest free from UK income tax.

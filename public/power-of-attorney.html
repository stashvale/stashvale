Power of Attorney (Information Only)

A Power of Attorney allows trusted people (attorneys) to make decisions on someone’s behalf, within limits set by law and the document. Neutral overview only — not legal advice.

Types & Roles (High Level)

  • Property & Financial: Decisions about money, property and financial matters.
  • Health & Welfare: Care and medical decisions (often only if capacity is lost).
  • Attorneys: Must act in the donor’s best interests and keep records.

Setting One Up (Overview)

  • Choose attorneys carefully (trust, capability, availability).
  • Forms must be completed, signed and witnessed correctly; registration may be required.
  • Fees, ID checks and timescales can apply.

Good Practice

  • Keep certified copies; store originals safely.
  • Review arrangements periodically.
  • Attorneys should keep clear records of decisions and transactions.

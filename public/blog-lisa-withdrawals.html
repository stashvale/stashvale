LISA Withdrawal Rules

Information only. This summary outlines when withdrawals can be made and when a withdrawal charge may apply.

When withdrawals are typically allowed without the early-withdrawal charge

  • Qualifying first-home purchase: Funds are paid to a conveyancer to complete a purchase that meets scheme conditions (e.g., property value caps and other criteria).
  • Later life: Withdrawals from the age specified in the scheme rules can usually be made without the early-withdrawal charge.
  • Other permitted circumstances: Certain limited situations may be covered in the rules. Providers will explain what applies and what evidence is required.

Process and timing

  • For first-home purchases, the conveyancer normally requests funds from the provider. Allow time for checks and transfers.
  • Provider processes and timescales vary. Some require specific forms and evidence.
  • If a transaction falls through, the rules may require funds to be returned to the LISA or the withdrawal charge may apply.

Other points to consider

  • With Stocks & Shares LISAs, investment values can rise and fall. The amount available can be higher or lower than contributions.
  • Tax rules and scheme limits can change in the future.
  • Check any residence, property usage and value criteria set by the scheme rules.

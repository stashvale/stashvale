Choosing the Right Monthly Income

This page outlines common factors people look at when deciding the monthly income on a Family Income Benefit policy. Information only — not financial advice.

Typical Factors

  • Essential outgoings: Housing costs, utilities, council tax, food, transport, childcare.
  • Debt commitments: Loan/credit agreements that would still need to be paid.
  • Existing resources: Employer benefits, state benefits, savings, investments, or other cover.
  • How long income might be needed: For example, to a youngest child’s independence age.
  • Indexation choices: Keeping income level vs. increasing each year to help with inflation (premiums usually change accordingly).
  • Affordability: Premiums must be paid to keep the policy in force; missed payments generally cause it to lapse.

Level vs. Indexed Income (General Information)

  • Level income: Simpler and cheaper than indexed options, but purchasing power can reduce over time with inflation.
  • Indexed income: Income rises each year by a set measure/cap; premiums typically increase in line with policy rules.

Term Alignment (Information Only)

People often align terms to practical milestones (e.g., remaining mortgage years or children reaching adulthood). Needs differ; this isn’t advice.

Life Insurance

A policy that pays a lump sum (or income) if you die during the policy term. This page explains the basics in plain English. Information only — not advice.

Key Types

  • Level term: Payout stays the same throughout the term.
  • Decreasing term: Payout reduces each year (often used with repayment mortgages).
  • Family Income Benefit (FIB): Regular income for the remaining term rather than a single lump sum.
  • Whole of Life: Designed to cover you for life (usually higher cost; separate page).

Premiums depend on age, term, amount of cover, smoker status, and medical underwriting by the insurer.

Things to Consider (Information Only)

  • Whether you want a lump sum or ongoing income for your family.
  • How long you’ll need cover (e.g. to align with a mortgage term).
  • Placing a policy in trust can help payouts go to the right people and may help with speed/estate issues.

Understanding the LISA Bonus

Information only. This guide explains how the Lifetime ISA (LISA) bonus is applied. It is not financial advice.

The Lifetime ISA is designed to help with a first-home purchase or later-life savings. Eligible contributions can receive a government bonus. Below is an overview of how the bonus works, when it is typically paid and practical points to be aware of.

Who can open a LISA?

  • Available to individuals within the age limits set in current rules.
  • Accounts can be Cash LISAs or Stocks & Shares LISAs. Investment values can rise and fall, so the amount available for a home purchase or later life may be higher or lower than contributions.

How the bonus is calculated

The bonus is a percentage of your eligible contributions paid into the LISA, up to the applicable annual limits set in the rules at the time.

  • The percentage rate is applied to money you pay in (not to investment growth).
  • If you contribute less than the annual limit, the bonus is calculated on the lower amount.
  • Transfers from other ISAs may have different treatment under current rules; providers will usually explain what qualifies.

When is the bonus paid?

Providers typically claim the bonus from HMRC and credit it to your LISA after your contribution is received. The timing can vary by provider and process, but it is generally added periodically (for example, monthly) following eligible payments.

Using the bonus

  • First home: Savings and any bonus may be used toward a qualifying first-home purchase that meets the scheme criteria (for example, purchase price caps and conveyancer involvement under current rules).
  • Later life: Withdrawals from a certain age under scheme rules are not subject to the early-withdrawal charge.

Important considerations

  • Withdrawals that do not meet scheme conditions can incur a government withdrawal charge, which may mean you get back less than you paid in.
  • Tax rules and allowances can change and their effect depends on individual circumstances.
  • If using a Stocks & Shares LISA, investments can fall as well as rise in value.
  • Account terms, bonus processing schedules and eligibility checks vary by provider.

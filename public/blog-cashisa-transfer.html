How to Transfer a Cash ISA (Step-by-Step)

Many providers allow transfers of existing Cash ISAs. This overview describes the typical process in plain English. It is information only and not personal advice.

Before you start

  • Check whether the receiving provider accepts transfers-in and whether it’s from the current tax year, a previous tax year, or both.
  • Review any penalties or loss of interest if you are moving from a fixed-term Cash ISA before maturity.
  • Confirm minimum balance and identification requirements.

Typical transfer steps

  1. Open/choose the new Cash ISA with a provider that accepts transfers-in.
  2. Fill out the transfer form provided by the new provider (paper or online). They should arrange the transfer for you.
  3. Do not withdraw the money yourself if you want to preserve ISA status — the transfer should be provider-to-provider.
  4. Wait for completion: timelines vary by provider and type of transfer.

Common considerations

  • Transferring current-year subscriptions may have specific rules; some people keep current-year funds together at one provider.
  • Partial transfers of previous-years’ ISA funds are often possible, subject to the receiving provider’s rules.
  • Rates can change; some accounts have limited-time offers.
  • Early exit from a fixed term can reduce interest; check terms before requesting a transfer.

After the transfer

Providers usually confirm when the transfer is complete. Statements and online banking should reflect the new balance and rate. Some people make a note of promotional end dates or fixed-term maturities for future reference.

Relevant Life Policy Basics

A Relevant Life Policy (RLP) is a type of employer-paid life assurance arranged on the life of an employee (including directors who are employees), with benefits normally payable to a nominated trust. This guide is information only — not financial, tax, legal or accounting advice.

What It’s Aimed To Do

  • Provide life cover for an individual employee outside a registered group scheme.
  • Allow the employer to pay premiums. Benefits are usually written in trust for the employee’s beneficiaries.
  • Offer a potential tax-efficient way to provide death-in-service-style cover for certain employees, subject to rules.

Eligibility depends on the insurer’s criteria and local rules. Sole traders covering themselves personally do not typically fit the usual use case.

High-Level Tax & Compliance Notes (Non-Exhaustive)

  • Tax treatment: Depends on purpose, policy terms and circumstance. Tax rules and HMRC practice can change.
  • Wholly & exclusively test: Employer deductibility may depend on this and other factors — professional advice is usually sought.
  • Trusts: Policies are commonly written in a discretionary trust so benefits can be paid to beneficiaries efficiently.
  • Pensions interaction: RLPs are separate from registered pension schemes; rules differ.

Typical Features (Information Only)

  • Cover type: Life cover; critical illness is usually not included in conventional RLP frameworks.
  • Sum assured: Often linked to a multiple of salary or total remuneration, subject to provider limits.
  • Term: Usually aligned to employment term or planned retirement age; terms vary by provider.
  • Underwriting: Medical and sometimes financial underwriting applies; non-disclosure can affect claims.

Who Commonly Considers RLPs?

  • Small companies wanting life cover for one or more employees or director-employees.
  • Firms without a group life scheme but wishing to provide death-in-service-style cover.
  • Businesses looking for an employer-paid arrangement where benefits are routed via trust.

Suitability is case-specific. Regulated, tax and legal advice is typically obtained before setting up a policy.

Important Reminders

  • This page explains general concepts only; it does not recommend a product.
  • Policy definitions, exclusions and limits vary by insurer — documents should be read carefully.
  • Tax treatment depends on individual and employer circumstances and may change.

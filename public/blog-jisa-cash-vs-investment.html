Cash vs Investment JISA

Information only. This guide explains general differences between Cash Junior ISAs and Stocks & Shares Junior ISAs. It does not recommend one option over another.

How they work (overview)

  • Cash JISA: Money is held as cash inside the JISA wrapper. The provider pays interest according to its terms.
  • Stocks & Shares JISA: Money is invested in funds or shares. The value can move up and down, sometimes noticeably over short periods.

Side-by-side comparison

Illustrative example: Some families choose a Cash JISA for stability. Others use a Stocks & Shares JISA to seek growth over many years. Both approaches involve trade-offs and neither is inherently “better” for everyone.

What to check with a provider

  • How interest is set (for Cash JISAs) and whether it is variable or fixed-term.
  • What platform/fund charges apply (for Stocks & Shares JISAs) and how they are taken.
  • How transfers work, typical timescales and any restrictions.
  • Minimum payments, ways to contribute and how to stop or change regular payments.
  • Reporting: online access, statements and how to track the account.

Common questions

Can you hold both types?

Under current rules, a child can hold one Cash JISA and one Stocks & Shares JISA at the same time. The overall annual allowance applies to total payments.

Can you change later?

Yes. Transfers between Cash and Stocks & Shares JISAs are generally possible. To retain the tax wrapper, the transfer is normally completed by providers.

Are returns guaranteed?

Returns are not guaranteed. Cash JISAs may pay variable interest unless a fixed term is specified. Stocks & Shares JISAs depend on market performance and can result in receiving back less than paid in.

Balanced points to consider

  • All JISAs are long-term accounts; funds are generally locked until age 18.
  • Charges, interest rates and investment options vary by provider and can change.
  • Investment values can go down as well as up; past performance is not a reliable indicator of future results.
  • Tax rules and allowances may change; effects depend on individual circumstances.

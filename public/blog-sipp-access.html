Accessing Your SIPP

There are several ways people access money from a SIPP. The right approach can depend on personal goals, provider options and current rules. This page explains the main concepts to help your understanding.

When access is usually possible

Pension legislation sets a minimum pension age for most people. Certain ill-health provisions can apply in limited situations. Providers explain their processes, options and timeframes when you request to take benefits.

Common ways of taking benefits

  • Lump sums and income: Many providers offer a tax-free element up to a permitted limit and taxable income thereafter. The mix and amounts vary by provider and legislation.
  • Drawdown (flexible income): Keep funds invested and take income as required, subject to provider rules. Investment risk remains; values can fall as well as rise.
  • Annuity (guaranteed income): Use some or all of the pot to buy an annuity that pays an income. Options and rates vary by provider and timing.
  • Combination: Some people use a mix of methods over time.

Balanced considerations

  • Tax: Withdrawals can be taxable; tax treatment depends on individual circumstances and can change.
  • Sustainability: Drawing income too quickly can reduce the pot; investment performance is not guaranteed.
  • Charges: There may be charges for transactions, drawdown, annuity purchase or ongoing administration.
  • Irreversibility: Some choices (for example, certain annuities) may be difficult or impossible to reverse.

Transfers and moving providers

Transferring a pension can be possible but may involve costs, risks and time out of the market. Some guarantees, features or protections may be lost on transfer. Providers explain options and any restrictions.

Questions to ask your provider

  • Which access options do you offer and what are the steps?
  • What are the charges and how are they taken?
  • How will tax be handled on withdrawals?
  • What are the timelines for paying funds to my bank?

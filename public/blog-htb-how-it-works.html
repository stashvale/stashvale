Help to Buy ISA: How It Worked

The Help to Buy ISA (HTB ISA) is closed to new applicants. Existing account holders may continue under scheme rules. This guide explains the core ideas in plain English.

What the scheme aimed to do

HTB ISAs were designed to support first-time buyers by adding a government bonus to eligible savings used for an eligible residential property purchase, where the scheme’s conditions were met.

Saving and managing the account

  • Existing holders typically continued to save with their provider, subject to the scheme’s contribution limits and provider terms.
  • Interest on cash held in the HTB ISA was paid by the provider according to the account rate.
  • Transfers between providers were usually possible, following both provider processes and the scheme’s transfer rules.

High-level eligibility concepts

  • First-time buyer status was required under the scheme’s definition.
  • Property price caps and UK residential use conditions applied.
  • Generally, a single HTB ISA bonus could be claimed per eligible purchase.

How the bonus was claimed

  • The provider did not automatically add the bonus. A conveyancer/solicitor usually claimed it during completion, following the scheme’s process.
  • When you closed your HTB ISA to use funds for a purchase, the provider typically issued a closing statement. The conveyancer used this as evidence for the bonus claim.
  • Forms, timeframes and evidence requirements were set by the scheme administrator and could be updated.

Key timing points

  • Deadlines applied to paying in, closing the account for a purchase, and the conveyancer’s claim submission.
  • If you were not buying immediately, you could usually continue saving within the scheme until you decided to purchase or close the account.

If plans changed

You could generally keep the account open (subject to provider terms) or close it and retain your savings without a bonus. Because the scheme is closed to new applicants, re-opening later may not have been possible.

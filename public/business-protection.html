Business Protection

Business protection insurance helps safeguard a company’s future if a key person, shareholder or director dies or becomes critically ill. This page is for information only and does not provide regulated financial advice.

What Is Business Protection?

Business protection is a type of life or critical illness insurance designed to protect the financial stability of a business if something unexpected happens to a key person. It ensures the company can continue trading, repay debts, buy out shares, or cover recruitment and training costs.

Key Types of Business Protection

  • Key Person Insurance: Pays a lump sum to the business if a key employee or director dies or is diagnosed with a critical illness. This can help replace lost profit or cover recruitment costs.
  • Shareholder / Partnership Protection: Provides funds for the remaining business owners to buy the shares of a deceased or critically ill shareholder, helping retain control and ensure smooth succession.
  • Relevant Life Policy: A tax-efficient life insurance policy arranged by a business on behalf of an employee or director, providing benefits to their family if they die while employed.

Policies are typically paid for by the business and can often be structured in a tax-efficient way — but advice from a regulated adviser is recommended before taking out a policy.

Why Businesses Consider Protection

  • To provide financial stability after the loss of a key revenue generator or decision-maker.
  • To protect shareholder control and ensure smooth ownership transitions.
  • To attract and retain staff by offering relevant life cover as a benefit.

The right type of protection will depend on your company’s size, structure and financial obligations.

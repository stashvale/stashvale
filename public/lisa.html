Lifetime ISA (LISA)

A Lifetime ISA (LISA) is a government-backed savings account designed to help you save for your first home or for retirement, with a valuable annual bonus. This page is for information only — not regulated financial advice.

How a LISA Works

A Lifetime ISA is available to individuals aged 18–39 and allows you to save up to £4,000 each tax year. The government adds a 25% bonus on top of what you save — up to £1,000 per year.

  • 🏠 First home: Use your LISA savings and bonus to buy your first property (up to £450,000) after 12 months.
  • 👵 Retirement: Withdraw from age 60 to boost your retirement savings tax-free.
  • 💸 Penalty if used otherwise: 25% withdrawal charge if funds are taken out for other purposes (you lose the bonus and more).

Eligibility & Key Rules

To open and contribute to a LISA, you must meet certain conditions:

  • ✔️ Aged 18–39 to open. You can contribute until age 50.
  • ✔️ Save up to £4,000 per tax year — part of your £20,000 total ISA allowance.
  • ✔️ Bonus is paid monthly on contributions and compounds over time.
  • ✔️ Property purchase must be via a mortgage and the property value under £450,000.
  • ✔️ Account must be open for at least 12 months before you can use it for a property purchase.

Things to Consider

  • ⚠️ Withdrawing early (for reasons other than home purchase or retirement) incurs a 25% charge.
  • ⚠️ A LISA should be part of a broader savings or retirement plan — not your only retirement solution.
  • ⚠️ Stocks & Shares LISAs offer higher growth potential but come with investment risk. Cash LISAs are lower risk but offer less potential growth.

Always seek regulated financial advice before making significant savings or investment decisions.

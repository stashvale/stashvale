Income Protection

Income protection is designed to pay a monthly benefit if illness or injury stops you working, after a waiting period. It helps cover regular bills while you recover. Information only — not financial advice.

What it is (Plain English)

  • You choose a monthly benefit (typically a % of pre-disability income) and how long the cover lasts.
  • If you meet the policy’s claim definition after the waiting period, the insurer pays a monthly benefit until you return to work, reach your benefit end date, or hit the policy’s limit.
  • Payments are usually tax-free for individuals (UK) but tax treatment can vary — check personal circumstances.

Key Features & Options

  • Deferred period: The wait before payments start (e.g., 1, 4, 8, 13, 26 weeks). Longer waits usually cost less.
  • Benefit duration: Short-term (e.g., up to 1–2 years per claim) or long-term (to end of term or selected cease age).
  • Claim definition: “Own occupation”, “suited occupation”, or “any occupation” (own-occ is generally the strongest).
  • Premium type: Guaranteed (fixed) or reviewable (can change). Some policies offer age-banded pricing.
  • Indexation: Option to increase benefit each year to help keep pace with inflation (premiums normally rise too).
  • Extras: Rehabilitation support, fracture cover, hospitalisation benefit — varies by insurer.

Things to Consider (Information Only)

  • Work & sick pay: How long would employer sick pay last, and how does that align with your deferred period?
  • Self-employed income: Proof of income at claim can matter — check how your policy verifies benefit.
  • Budget & priorities: Deferred period, benefit level, and duration have a big impact on cost.
  • Underwriting: Health, lifestyle, and occupation can affect premiums, exclusions, or if cover is offered.
  • Existing cover: Check if you have any employer or personal cover already to avoid overlaps.

Deeper Detail (For those who want more)

  • Proportionate/rehabilitation benefits: Some policies pay partial benefits if you return part-time or to a lower-paid role.
  • Evidence at claim: Medical evidence and income verification are typically required.
  • Cease age & term: Common cease ages include 60, 65, or state pension age; premiums reflect the risk to that age.
  • Tax & structure: Personal vs business policies can be taxed/treated differently — seek professional guidance.

When to Review Your Cover

Family Income Benefit needs can change over time. This page lists common moments people use as prompts to check whether their policy still fits their circumstances. Information only — not financial advice.

Common Triggers People Consider

  • Children growing up: As dependants become financially independent, required income and term may change.
  • New child or dependants: Household outgoings and term considerations can increase.
  • Job or income changes: Budget and benefit level may be revisited.
  • New debts or clearing debts: Overall affordability and protection needs can shift.
  • Housing changes: Moving, remortgaging, or rent changes may affect the suitable income level.
  • Relationship changes: Marriage, separation, or new beneficiaries can require updates (e.g., trusts/beneficiary nominations).
  • Health disclosures: Applications and changes must follow insurer rules; non-disclosure can affect cover.

Practical Checks (Information Only)

  • Confirm the remaining policy term still lines up with your goals.
  • Check whether the income is level or indexed and still appropriate.
  • Confirm premiums are affordable. Policies usually end if premiums are missed.
  • Make sure beneficiary/trust details remain up to date.
  • Be aware that replacing or changing cover can have costs or underwriting considerations.

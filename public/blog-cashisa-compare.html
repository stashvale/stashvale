Cash ISA vs Regular Savings (Information Only)

This guide sets out general differences between Cash ISAs and regular savings accounts in plain English. It is not advice and does not recommend one over the other.

Tax treatment

  • Cash ISA: Interest is tax-free within ISA rules and allowances.
  • Regular savings: Interest may be taxable, though some people benefit from a Personal Savings Allowance (PSA). PSA levels and rules can change.

Tax depends on personal circumstances and may change in future.

Access and flexibility

  • Both Cash ISAs and regular accounts can be easy access or fixed term, depending on the product.
  • Some fixed accounts (in both categories) may charge penalties for early withdrawals.
  • Providers set their own withdrawal rules and minimum balances.

Rates and promotions

  • Rates vary by provider and product type. They can change, especially on easy-access accounts.
  • Introductory rates and limited-time promotions may apply and can end.
  • Fixed terms may offer higher rates in return for committing funds.

Other practical differences

  • Cash ISAs sit within ISA rules (e.g., annual allowance, transfer options, eligibility). Regular savings do not use the ISA allowance.
  • Some people consider whether they’re already using their PSA before deciding if ISA tax-free status is important for them.
  • Charges are not usually a feature of standard cash savings; always check provider terms.

Summary (neutral)

A Cash ISA provides tax-free interest within ISA rules, while a regular savings account may be simpler if tax isn’t a factor for the individual. Many people review the rate, access needs, ISA allowances, and any fixed-term conditions before opening an account.

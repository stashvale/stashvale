Help to Buy ISA: Deadlines & Bonus Timelines

This page outlines common timing points for existing Help to Buy ISA savers. It is not a substitute for your provider’s terms or your conveyancer’s guidance.

Typical timing points

  • Paying in: Existing holders could usually continue to contribute within scheme rules until they chose to purchase or close the account.
  • Closing for a purchase: When you were ready to buy, you generally asked your provider to close the HTB ISA and issue a closing statement for your conveyancer.
  • Bonus claim at completion: The conveyancer normally claimed the government bonus as part of completion, using the closing statement and any required forms.

Co-ordinating with your conveyancer

  • Conveyancers follow the scheme’s claim process. They may ask for documents by specific dates so completion isn’t delayed.
  • If completion dates change, your conveyancer can explain how that affects the timeline for the claim.
  • If you switch provider close to purchase, allow time for any transfer or closure steps.

If timings shift

Property purchases can move. If expected completion dates change, your provider or conveyancer may need updated documents. Asking early about their cut-off points can help avoid last-minute issues.

Mortgages (Information Only)

This page explains mortgage basics in plain English. It is general information only — not financial advice. Mortgage products are regulated in the UK. Speak to a regulated adviser or lender before making decisions.

How a Mortgage Works

  • Loan secured on a property: You borrow money to buy a home; the lender takes a legal charge over it.
  • Interest types: Fixed (rate set for a period) or variable/tracker (can move up/down).
  • Term: The overall length of the mortgage (e.g. 25–35 years). Monthly payments aim to repay interest and sometimes capital.
  • Repayment vs interest-only: Repayment aims to clear the loan by the end. Interest-only needs a separate plan to repay the capital.
  • Fees & charges: Product fees, valuation, legal costs, early-repayment charges may apply.

Lending criteria, affordability assessments and property valuations influence what you can borrow.

Key Terms Explained

  • Loan-to-Value (LTV): The loan as a % of the property value (e.g. 85% LTV).
  • Initial deal period: The fixed/discount/tracker period (e.g. 2 or 5 years) before moving to the lender’s standard variable rate.
  • Early Repayment Charge (ERC): A fee if you repay/switch during the initial period.
  • Porting: Moving your existing deal to a new property (subject to lender rules/affordability).
  • Remortgaging: Switching to a new product or lender, usually when a deal ends.

Things to Consider (Information Only)

  • Rate type and how comfortable you are with potential rate changes.
  • Fees, overall cost over the initial deal and whole term — not just the headline rate.
  • Your budget, emergency savings and protection needs (e.g., income protection/life cover separate pages).
  • Future plans: moving home, overpaying, or needing flexibility.

This is not advice. Suitability depends on personal circumstances and may change over time.

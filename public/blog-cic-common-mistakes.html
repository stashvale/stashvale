Common Critical Illness Cover Mistakes to Be Aware Of

Information only — not financial advice. This guide highlights typical areas where expectations can differ from policy wording, so you know what to look for when reading the documents.

1) Assuming “Named Conditions” Always Pay

Coverage depends on the exact definition, severity and any listed exclusions. For example, some early-stage cancers may not meet the policy criteria for a full payout. Many policies include partial payments for specified lower-severity diagnoses.

2) Not Checking Exclusions & Special Terms

Underwriting outcomes can include premium loadings or exclusions (for example, on certain conditions). It’s important to read any special terms shown on your policy schedule so you understand how cover applies.

3) Overlooking Definition Updates Between Products

Insurers update wordings over time. A newer product could have different definitions or features than an older one. This doesn’t mean one is “better” in every respect — it’s simply different. The wording for your specific policy is the reference point.

Myth vs Reality

  • Myth: “Children’s cover is always included.”
    Reality: Some policies include it, others don’t, and limits vary widely.
  • Myth: “Partial payments mean I’m covered for everything.”
    Reality: Partial payments apply only to listed conditions and severities.

Key Questions to Ask Yourself

  • Do you know which conditions and severities your policy defines as claimable?
  • Are there exclusions or special terms shown on your schedule?
  • Would a lump sum help reduce financial pressure during serious illness?

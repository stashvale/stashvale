When to Review Your Life Insurance

Information only — not financial advice. Many people choose to look over their life insurance from time to time to check it still aligns with their situation, especially after major life changes.

Common Triggers People Consider

  • New home or mortgage: Repayment amounts and terms can change over time.
  • Marriage, separation or children: Household needs and priorities may shift.
  • Income changes: Salary rises or reduced hours might alter what feels appropriate.
  • Debts paid down: Some people find their cover amount or term feels high relative to remaining commitments.

What People Typically Check

  • Amount of cover: Whether the lump sum or income would still support key outgoings.
  • Type of cover: If level, decreasing or family income benefit still fits household preferences.
  • Policy term: Whether the end date still lines up with needs (e.g., mortgage finish date).
  • Beneficiaries or trust arrangements: Ensuring paperwork reflects current wishes.

Insurer definitions, exclusions and processes vary. Check the policy schedule and Key Facts for the definitive position.

Myth vs Reality

  • Myth: “If nothing big changed this year, there’s no point looking.”
    Reality: Some people prefer a light check-in periodically to stay familiar with what they have.
  • Myth: “All life insurance is the same.”
    Reality: Features and definitions differ by insurer, and by product type.

Key Questions to Ask Yourself

  • Would your family still be able to manage ongoing costs with the current payout?
  • Has your mortgage or other borrowing changed since you took out the policy?
  • Do your beneficiaries and any trust arrangements reflect your current wishes?

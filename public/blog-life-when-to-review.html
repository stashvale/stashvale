When Should You Review Your Life Insurance?

Life doesn’t stand still — and neither should your life insurance. It’s good practice to review your policy regularly to ensure it still meets your needs. This article is for information purposes only and not financial advice.

Major Life Events That Might Trigger a Review

Many people take out a policy and never revisit it, but that can mean their cover no longer matches their circumstances. Consider a review after:

  • Buying a property or remortgaging: A larger mortgage might require increased cover.
  • Marriage or divorce: New responsibilities — or fewer — can affect how much protection you need.
  • Having children: Growing your family usually increases the amount of financial support required.
  • Career changes: Promotions or job changes can alter income, lifestyle, and future needs.
  • Starting a business: Personal protection might need to be supplemented by business cover.

Other Reasons to Check Your Cover

  • Inflation: Over time, the value of your payout could erode. Some policies offer increasing cover options.
  • Changing health: If your health improves, you might find more competitive premiums elsewhere.
  • Policy type or length: Your original choice (e.g. decreasing vs. level term) might no longer be suitable.

How Often Should You Review?

Most experts suggest reviewing life insurance every 2 to 3 years — or sooner if you experience any of the major life events listed above.

Even if nothing major has changed, it’s worth checking that your policy still aligns with your financial goals and responsibilities.

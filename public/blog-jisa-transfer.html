How to Transfer a Junior ISA

Information only. This guide outlines how Junior ISA transfers typically work, including moving from a Child Trust Fund (CTF) to a Junior ISA. Processes vary by provider.

Before you start

  • Check the child’s eligibility and confirm the existing account type (Cash JISA, Stocks & Shares JISA, or CTF).
  • Ask the new provider about their transfer pack, ID requirements and typical timescales.
  • To retain the tax-advantaged status, transfers are usually completed by providers. Withdrawing money and paying it back in later may not preserve the wrapper.

Typical transfer steps

1) Choose the receiving provider. Review their charges, available investments (if applicable) and online access features.
2) Request a transfer form. Provide details of the existing account and whether you want to transfer all or (if permitted) part of the balance.
3) Provider-to-provider transfer. The receiving provider coordinates with the existing provider. Cash JISAs usually move as cash. Stocks & Shares JISAs may be sold and repurchased (exact process varies).
4) Confirmation. When complete, the receiving provider confirms the new account is active and the transfer has finished.

Transferring from a Child Trust Fund (CTF)

  • Under current rules, it is generally possible to move a CTF into a JISA. After the transfer, the JISA continues for the child until age 18.
  • Ask providers about any differences in timings or forms compared with a JISA-to-JISA transfer.
  • While the transfer is in progress, contributions may be paused until the new account is active (provider processes vary).

Timing, charges and what can vary

  • Timescales: These depend on both providers and the type of assets involved.
  • Charges: Platform charges, fund charges and any exit or transfer-out fees depend on providers and products.
  • Market movement: For Stocks & Shares JISAs, values can move during the transfer process.

Practical tips (neutral)

  • Keep copies of forms and confirmations.
  • Ask how you will be notified when regular payments should resume.
  • If moving investments, ask whether assets are transferred “in specie” (where available) or by selling and repurchasing.

FAQs

Can you transfer part of a JISA?

Some providers allow partial transfers; others require full transfers. Check the receiving provider’s rules and the current regulations.

Will the child’s allowance be affected?

The annual allowance is separate from transfers. Providers explain how they track payments in the tax year.

Can a CTF be kept instead?

Some families keep a CTF until maturity; others transfer to a JISA. Providers can explain differences in features and processes.

Balanced points to consider

  • Provider processes vary. Ask about forms, identification, transfer timescales and whether payments should pause.
  • For Stocks & Shares JISAs, investment values can fall as well as rise; you may receive back less than has been paid in.
  • Tax rules and allowances can change; effects depend on individual circumstances.

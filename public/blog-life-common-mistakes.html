Common Life Insurance Mistakes to Avoid

Life insurance can be straightforward, but small oversights can make a big difference. Here are some of the most frequent mistakes — and how to avoid them. Information only – not advice.

1. Underestimating How Much Cover You Need

One of the biggest mistakes is choosing a payout that doesn’t fully meet your family’s needs. Think beyond the mortgage — factor in childcare, education, ongoing living costs, and inflation.

2. Choosing the Wrong Policy Type

Level term, decreasing term, and family income benefit all serve different purposes. Choosing the wrong one could mean you’re either overpaying or not properly protected.

3. Forgetting to Review Regularly

Life circumstances change. A policy you took out 10 years ago might not reflect your current financial situation. Regular reviews ensure your cover remains suitable.

4. Not Writing Your Policy Into Trust

If your policy isn’t written into trust, the payout may be delayed by probate or counted as part of your estate, potentially increasing inheritance tax liability.

5. Cancelling Without a Replacement

If you cancel a policy before setting up a new one, you could be left without cover — and premiums may be higher if you reapply later due to age or health changes.

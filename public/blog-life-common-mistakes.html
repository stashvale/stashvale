Common Life Insurance Mistakes to Be Aware Of

Information only — not financial advice. This guide highlights typical areas where people sometimes find their cover doesn’t work as expected. It’s designed to help you ask the right questions when reading policy documents.

1) Cover Amount That No Longer Matches the Need

Life changes can mean the original amount of cover feels high or low for current circumstances. Some people look at big outgoings (like a mortgage or childcare) and consider whether their existing payout would still provide the support they want for loved ones.

2) Term Length That Doesn’t Line Up

Policies run for a set period. If the term ends earlier than key commitments (e.g., years left on a mortgage), the timing may not align with expectations. Conversely, some find they continue paying for longer than needed if debts or obligations reduce faster than expected.

3) Not Understanding the Policy Type

Level, decreasing and family income benefit have different purposes. For example, decreasing cover usually reduces in line with a repayment mortgage, while FIB pays a regular income rather than a lump sum. Checking the policy summary helps clarify how payments would be made.

4) Paperwork and Beneficiaries Not Up to Date

Some people periodically check that beneficiaries and any trust paperwork still reflect current wishes. Where a trust is used, it may help benefits reach intended recipients more smoothly. Tax and estate outcomes depend on how the policy and any trust are arranged, so documents are key.

Myth vs Reality

  • Myth: “All claims are handled the same way.”
    Reality: Processes and definitions differ by insurer, so the policy wording matters.
  • Myth: “Putting a policy in trust is always the right answer.”
    Reality: Trusts are a common arrangement, but suitability depends on individual circumstances and how documents are set up.

Help to Buy ISA vs LISA vs Cash ISA

A plain-English comparison of three ISA types. It outlines common features and differences at a high level.

Overview

Feature (summary) Help to Buy ISA Lifetime ISA (LISA) Cash ISA
Availability Closed to new applicants; existing holders may continue within scheme rules. Open to eligible applicants subject to age and other criteria set by the scheme and providers. Widely available from many providers (cash savings).
Main purpose Support first-time buyers via a government bonus when used for an eligible purchase. Save/invest for a first home or later life, with potential government bonus under scheme rules. Tax-efficient home for cash savings; no government bonus.
Bonus / incentives Bonus usually claimed by a conveyancer at completion (subject to scheme limits and rules). Bonus usually added by the provider to qualifying subscriptions, subject to scheme rules. No bonus; interest depends on provider rates.
Using funds For an eligible property purchase under scheme conditions; closing statement used for claim. Usually for first-home purchase under scheme rules or for later life withdrawals; early withdrawals may incur a charge. Withdraw any time per provider terms; no bonus implications.
Transfers Possible under provider and scheme processes. Possible; rules apply when moving money in/out of a LISA. Common and generally straightforward between providers.

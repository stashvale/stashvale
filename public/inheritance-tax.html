Inheritance Tax (IHT) Basics

IHT generally looks at the value of an estate and how rules, exemptions and allowances apply at the time. This page is information only — not tax or legal advice.

Core Concepts (High Level)

  • Estate value: Assets minus allowable liabilities at the relevant date.
  • Thresholds & allowances: Frameworks exist and may change over time.
  • Transfers to spouse/civil partner: Often benefit from exemptions, with potential transfer of unused allowances (subject to rules).
  • Exemptions & reliefs: Examples include charitable gifts and certain business/agricultural reliefs (conditions apply).

Lifetime Gifts (Overview)

Some gifts may be exempt immediately; others can be considered if made within certain timeframes before death. Outcomes depend on timing, value and the rules in force at the time.

Records & Practicalities

  • Keep records of assets, liabilities and gifts (dates, amounts, context).
  • Valuations and paperwork help evidence positions taken on any forms.
  • Where personal tax planning is needed, many people seek professional advice.

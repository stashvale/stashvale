Equity Release (Information Only)

Equity release products allow homeowners to access value tied up in their property, usually later in life. This is a neutral overview — not advice or a recommendation.

Common Types

  • Lifetime Mortgages: A loan secured on your home, typically with interest rolled up, repaid when you die or move into long-term care.
  • Home Reversion Plans: Selling a percentage of your home to a provider in exchange for a lump sum or income, while retaining the right to live there.

Neutral Considerations

  • Impact on inheritance and the value left to beneficiaries.
  • Effect on means-tested benefits and tax position.
  • Fees, interest costs and provider terms.
  • Alternatives people consider (downsizing, other borrowing, or support).

These products are regulated and complex. For personal recommendations, consider regulated financial advice.

Practicalities

  • Property criteria, valuations and legal work apply.
  • Early repayment charges and portability rules may exist.
  • Independent legal advice is commonly required.

Combined vs Standalone Critical Illness

Information only — not advice. Some policies combine life insurance and critical illness in one plan. Others provide critical illness on its own. The points below outline typical differences.

Combined (life + CI) — overview

  • One policy and premium; a benefit may be paid on a covered CI diagnosis or death (as per the wording).
  • In many combined plans, a CI claim can reduce or remove remaining life cover (policy-specific).
  • Some people prefer the simplicity of a single plan.

Standalone CI — overview

  • Only CI benefits are included — no life cover.
  • A full CI claim often ends the policy once paid (check the terms).
  • Gives flexibility to arrange life cover separately if wanted.

Other variations

Some products offer “enhanced” options with broader condition lists or partial payments. Terms, costs and definitions vary by insurer and version.

Things people often compare

  • Whether a CI claim affects life cover on a combined plan.
  • Scope of covered conditions and how each is defined.
  • Budget, term length, and whether children’s cover is included or optional.

Product terms differ by provider. This page is general information, not personal advice.

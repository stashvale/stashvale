Cash ISA

A Cash ISA is a tax-free savings account available to UK residents aged 16 or over. It allows you to earn interest without paying tax on it, making it a simple, low-risk way to grow your savings. This page is for information only – it is not financial advice.

How a Cash ISA Works

A Cash ISA works much like a regular savings account, but with one key difference: any interest you earn is completely free from UK income tax. Each tax year, you get an ISA allowance — £20,000 for the 2025/26 tax year — which you can split between different ISA types (cash, stocks & shares, Lifetime ISA, etc.).

  • 💷 Tax-free interest: All interest earned within a Cash ISA is free from income tax.
  • 📅 Annual allowance: Up to £20,000 total ISA contributions across all ISA types.
  • 🔄 Flexible or fixed: Choose between easy-access accounts or fixed-rate deals for higher interest.

Types of Cash ISAs

  • Easy-access Cash ISA: Withdraw money anytime, but usually with lower interest rates.
  • Fixed-rate Cash ISA: Lock your money away for a set term (e.g. 1–5 years) in exchange for higher interest.
  • Flexible Cash ISA: Withdraw and replace money within the same tax year without losing your allowance.

Things to Consider

  • ⚠️ Interest rates on Cash ISAs are often lower than inflation, so your money may lose value in real terms.
  • 💡 If you’re unlikely to use your full Personal Savings Allowance, a Cash ISA may not offer a big advantage.
  • 🔁 You can transfer your ISA to another provider to chase better rates — but always check for penalties.

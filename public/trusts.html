Trusts (Information Only)

A trust is a legal arrangement where assets are held by trustees for the benefit of others under set terms. This page is general information only — not legal, tax or financial advice.

Key Roles

  • Settlor: Puts assets into the trust and sets intentions.
  • Trustees: Manage the trust in line with the terms and relevant law.
  • Beneficiaries: May benefit from the trust under the terms.

Common Types (High Level)

  • Bare trusts: Beneficiaries are absolutely entitled to the assets.
  • Discretionary trusts: Trustees have discretion over distributions within the class of beneficiaries.
  • Interest in possession: Beneficiaries may have a right to income, with capital passing later.

Neutral Considerations

  • Administration, record-keeping and potential reporting/registration requirements.
  • Tax treatment depends on the type of trust, the assets and current law.
  • Professional advice is often sought due to legal and tax complexity.

If you need personal guidance, consider a qualified solicitor and/or tax professional.

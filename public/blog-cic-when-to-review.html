When to Review Critical Illness Cover

Information only — not financial advice. Some people choose to look over their CIC from time to time to check the level of cover, condition wording and any partial payment features still suit their needs.

Life Events That Often Prompt a Check-In

  • New home or new mortgage: Financial responsibilities may change.
  • Marriage, separation or children: Household needs evolve.
  • Changes in income: Affordability preferences can shift over time.
  • Product developments: Newer policies sometimes include broader definitions or extra features.

Areas People Commonly Review

  • Amount of cover: Whether the payout would still help with key costs during a serious illness.
  • Definitions & exclusions: Understanding the exact wording for major conditions.
  • Partial payments: Whether the policy includes them and for which conditions.
  • Children’s cover or added services: Some products include extras; availability varies.

Your policy schedule and Key Facts remain the definitive source for what is and isn’t covered.

Myth vs Reality

  • Myth: “If I have CIC, I’m covered for any serious illness.”
    Reality: Cover depends on the list of defined conditions and their wording in the policy.
  • Myth: “Older policies are always worse.”
    Reality: Older and newer policies can differ; some older wordings may be broader in specific areas, others narrower.

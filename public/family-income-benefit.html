Family Income Benefit (FIB)

Family Income Benefit (FIB) is a type of life insurance that pays a **regular monthly income** to your loved ones if you pass away during the policy term. It’s designed to provide ongoing financial support rather than a single lump sum. Information only — not advice.

How Family Income Benefit Works

Unlike a standard life insurance policy that pays out a one-off lump sum, Family Income Benefit pays a **tax-free monthly income** for the remainder of the policy term. This means your family can plan their finances more easily and receive steady support instead of needing to manage a single large payment.

For example, if you took out a 25-year policy and passed away in year 15, the policy would pay a monthly income for the remaining 10 years.

Key Features

  • ✅ Provides **ongoing monthly income** rather than a lump sum.
  • ✅ Helps cover regular living costs such as rent, bills, childcare, and education.
  • ✅ Typically more **cost-effective** than equivalent lump-sum life cover.
  • ✅ Can be written in trust to help the money go directly to the people you choose.

FIB vs. Lump-Sum Life Insurance

Both options aim to protect your family financially — but they do it differently:

  • FIB: Regular monthly payments until the end of the policy term. Great for replacing income or covering everyday costs.
  • Lump-sum cover: A single payment. Useful for clearing a mortgage, paying off debts, or leaving an inheritance.

Many people combine the two — for example, a lump sum to repay the mortgage and FIB to replace lost income.

Things to Consider (Information Only)

  • How long you’ll need income support (e.g. until children reach adulthood).
  • Whether the amount of monthly income will keep pace with inflation.
  • Placing the policy in trust can help speed up payment and avoid inheritance tax issues.
  • Premiums vary based on age, health, smoking status, and the level of income chosen.

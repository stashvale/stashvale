Family Income Benefit (FIB)

Family Income Benefit is a type of life insurance that pays a regular income to your chosen beneficiaries if you die during the policy term — rather than a single lump sum. Information only — not advice or a personal recommendation. Rules, products and tax treatment can change.

How it Works

  • You choose the term (length of cover) and the amount of monthly or annual benefit.
  • If you die during the term, the policy pays the agreed benefit amount each month (or year) to your chosen beneficiaries until the end of the remaining term.
  • Payments stop at the end of the term — it is not a lifetime benefit.

Example: A 20-year FIB policy paying £2,000/month could pay £240,000 over 10 years if a claim occurred halfway through the term.

Key Features & Options

  • Level or Indexed Benefits: The payment amount can remain fixed or rise each year to help keep pace with inflation.
  • Joint or Single Life: Policies can cover one person or two (commonly first-death basis).
  • Guaranteed Premiums: Premiums are usually fixed, though indexing increases both the benefit and the premium.
  • Trusts & Beneficiaries: Placing the policy in trust can help with faster payouts and clearer beneficiary arrangements.

Things to Consider (Information Only)

  • Purpose of cover: Many people align the term with the period of financial dependence (e.g. until children are financially independent).
  • Inflation: A fixed income may lose purchasing power over time — indexation can help but increases cost.
  • Underwriting & disclosure: Accurate health and lifestyle information is required. Non-disclosure can impact claims.
  • Trusts: Consider legal or regulated advice if unsure about trust structures.
  • Affordability & lapse: Missing premiums can cause the policy to lapse and cover to stop.
  • Tax position: Benefits are not usually subject to UK income tax but may form part of the estate for inheritance tax purposes unless arranged otherwise.
  • Reviews over time: Life changes (e.g. children, mortgage changes, income shifts) may prompt a review of cover.

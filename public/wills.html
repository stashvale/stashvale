Wills & Estate Planning Basics

This guide explains, in neutral terms, what a will generally does, common roles involved, and how a will can fit into wider estate planning.

What a will generally does

  • States who is intended to receive assets (beneficiaries) and in what proportions or on what terms.
  • Names executors who are responsible for administering the estate in line with the will and relevant law.
  • May include guardianship wishes for dependants and instructions about personal items or charitable gifts.
  • Can interact with other arrangements (for example, property ownership forms or nominated beneficiaries on certain accounts).

Key roles and terms

  • Testator/testatrix: the person making the will.
  • Executor: the person(s) responsible for carrying out the will and managing the estate administration.
  • Beneficiary: a person or organisation intended to receive assets from the estate.
  • Witnesses & signing: specific witnessing and signing rules normally apply; processes vary by jurisdiction and can change.

Keeping a will up to date

  • People often review a will after major life events (for example, marriage, civil partnership, divorce, birth of a child, or acquiring property).
  • Storing the will safely and making sure executors know where to find it can help the administration process.
  • Some assets may pass outside the will (for example, certain jointly-owned property or accounts with named beneficiaries).

How a will fits into wider planning

A will is one element of estate planning. Other elements may include powers of attorney, basic cash-flow planning, and understanding tax rules that may apply to an estate. The relevance of each depends on personal circumstances.

Frequently asked questions (general)

Do I need a solicitor?

Some people choose to use a solicitor or professional will writer to help ensure documents meet legal requirements. Others prefer to draft their own. This is a personal choice.

What about tax?

Tax rules can change and depend on individual circumstances. If tax is a concern, people often seek guidance from a qualified professional.

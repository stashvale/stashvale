Using a LISA for Your First Home

Information only. This is a general overview of how LISA funds may be used for a first-home purchase under current scheme rules.

Before you start

  • Check you meet the scheme’s eligibility criteria for first-time buyers and property value limits set in the rules.
  • Confirm with your provider and conveyancer how funds will be requested and paid.
  • Allow for timing: providers usually need notice to request bonus payment and transfer funds to your conveyancer.

Typical steps

1) Build your LISA savings
Make eligible contributions. If using a Stocks & Shares LISA, remember investments can go down as well as up.

2) Instruct a conveyancer/solicitor
They will usually request funds from your LISA provider and ensure conditions are met for a qualifying purchase.

3) Provider processes the request
The provider checks eligibility and arranges transfer. The government bonus credited to your LISA is included in the amount released if conditions are met.

4) Completion
Funds are applied toward the purchase as part of the conveyancing process.

Key points to watch

  • There may be a minimum period between opening a LISA and using it for a qualifying purchase. Check current rules and provider terms.
  • If the purchase does not complete or conditions are not met, the money may need to be returned to the LISA. Non-qualifying withdrawals can incur the government withdrawal charge.
  • Property value caps, residency and other criteria apply under scheme rules. Providers and conveyancers will set out what they require.
Remember: Scheme rules and tax treatment can change. Providers’ processes and timeframes vary, and additional checks may be required during conveyancing.
